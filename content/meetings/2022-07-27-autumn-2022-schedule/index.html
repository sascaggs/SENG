Beginning in September and ending in December, SENG is holding seven hybrid meetings.

The focus of our meetings this term is on building confidence in our network analysis methods. We will do this by workshopping a lot and hosting fewer seminar style talks. We want our members to end this term feeling even better about using and teaching network analysis in the future.

After a welcome meeting on 9/9, we will meet to discuss research methods and design, focusing on methods used by social scientists to collect complete and personal networks. We will follow this meeting with a refresher on how to work with networks in R. The next two meetings will involve a deep dive into Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs). In these meetings we will use simulation, workshop analyses that are members are currently developing, and learn how to interpret and summarize multiple models. The final two meetings will delve into some advanced topics including Bayesian inference and community detection.
